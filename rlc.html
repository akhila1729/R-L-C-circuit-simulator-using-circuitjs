CIRCUIT SIMULATOR for a series R-L-C circuit

A series R-L-C circuit has resistor(R), inductor(L) and capacitor(C) connected in series. It is a oscillatory circuit like L-C circuit but with damping due to resistor. The current in the circuit is same at every point but voltage differs along the circuit. It is also called second-order circuit as after applying kirchhoff's voltage law,we get a second order differential equation to be solved for voltage and current. A detailed derivation of the solution of the circuit is given below.



Mathematical circuit analysis







Mathematical circuit analysis of R-L-C circuit



In the circuit, R,L and C are in series. Applying kirchhoff's voltage law in the circuit,we get
VR + VL + VC = V(t)
where VR, VL and VC are the voltages across R, L and C respectively and V(t) is the time-varying voltage from the source.
Substituting VR=IR , and yields

R I ( t ) + L d I ( t ) d t + V ( 0 ) + 1 C ∫ 0 t I ( τ ) d τ = V ( t ) . {\displaystyle RI(t)+L{\frac {dI(t)}{dt}}+V(0)+{\frac {1}{C}}\int _{0}^{t}I(\tau )\,d\tau =V(t)\,.}
Here we are considering V(t) to be constant dc voltage V. Taking the time derivative and dividing by L leads to the following second order differential equation:
d 2 d t 2 I ( t ) + R L d d t I ( t ) + 1 L C I ( t ) = 0 . {\displaystyle {\frac {d^{2}}{dt^{2}}}I(t)+{\frac {R}{L}}{\frac {d}{dt}}I(t)+{\frac {1}{LC}}I(t)=0\,.}
This can be expressed as:
d 2 d t 2 I ( t ) + 2 α d d t I ( t ) + ω 0 2 I ( t ) = 0 . {\displaystyle {\frac {d^{2}}{dt^{2}}}I(t)+2\alpha {\frac {d}{dt}}I(t)+\omega _{0}^{2}I(t)=0\,.}
alpha and w0 are both in units of angular frequency. Alpha is called the neper frequency, or attenuation, and is a measure of how fast the transient response of the circuit will die away after the stimulus has been removed. w0 is the angular resonance frequency.
In series RLC circuit,
α = R 2 L ω 0 = 1 L C . {\displaystyle {\begin{aligned}\alpha &={\frac {R}{2L}}\\\omega _{0}&={\frac {1}{\sqrt {LC}}}\,.\end{aligned}}}
Now we define a new parameter called damping parameter,
ζ = α ω 0 . {\displaystyle \zeta ={\frac {\alpha }{\omega _{0}}}\,.}
In the case of series RLC circuit damping parameter is given by,
ζ = R 2 C L . {\displaystyle \zeta ={\frac {R}{2}}{\sqrt {\frac {C}{L}}}\,.}
The differential equation has the characteristic equation:
s 2 + 2 α s + ω 0 2 = 0 . {\displaystyle s^{2}+2\alpha s+\omega _{0}^{2}=0\,.}
The roots of the equation are:
s 1 = − α + α 2 − ω 0 2 s 2 = − α − α 2 − ω 0 2 . {\displaystyle {\begin{aligned}s_{1}&=-\alpha +{\sqrt {\alpha ^{2}-\omega _{0}^{2}}}\\s_{2}&=-\alpha -{\sqrt {\alpha ^{2}-\omega _{0}^{2}}}\,.\end{aligned}}}
The general solution of the differential equation is,
I ( t ) = A 1 e s 1 t + A 2 e s 2 t . {\displaystyle I(t)=A_{1}e^{s_{1}t}+A_{2}e^{s_{2}t}\,.}
where A1 and A2 are constants determined by the boundary conditions.
Overdamped response
This is when damping parameter is greater than one.
I ( t ) = A 1 e − ω 0 ( ζ + ζ 2 − 1 ) t + A 2 e − ω 0 ( ζ − ζ 2 − 1 ) t . {\displaystyle I(t)=A_{1}e^{-\omega _{0}\left(\zeta +{\sqrt {\zeta ^{2}-1}}\right)t}+A_{2}e^{-\omega _{0}\left(\zeta -{\sqrt {\zeta ^{2}-1}}\right)t}\,.}
It is the decay of the transient current without oscillation.
Underdamped response
This response is when damping parameter is less than 1.
I ( t ) = B 1 e − α t cos ⁡ ( ω d t ) + B 2 e − α t sin ⁡ ( ω d t ) . {\displaystyle I(t)=B_{1}e^{-\alpha t}\cos(\omega _{\mathrm {d} }t)+B_{2}e^{-\alpha t}\sin(\omega _{\mathrm {d} }t)\,.}
By applying trignometric identities the two sinusoids may be expressed as a single sinusoid with a phase shift,
I ( t ) = B 3 e − α t sin ⁡ ( ω d t + φ ) . {\displaystyle I(t)=B_{3}e^{-\alpha t}\sin(\omega _{\mathrm {d} }t+\varphi )\,.}
ω d = ω 0 2 − α 2 = ω 0 1 − ζ 2 . {\displaystyle \omega _{\mathrm {d} }={\sqrt {\omega _{0}^{2}-\alpha ^{2}}}=\omega _{0}{\sqrt {1-\zeta ^{2}}}\,.}
This is called the damped resonance frequency or the damped natural frequency. It is the frequency the circuit will naturally oscillate at if not driven by an external source.
Critically damped response
In critically damped response,damping parameter is 1.
I ( t ) = D 1 t e − α t + D 2 e − α t . {\displaystyle I(t)=D_{1}te^{-\alpha t}+D_{2}e^{-\alpha t}\,.}
where D1 and D2 are determined by boundary conditions.
