CIRCUIT SIMULATOR for a series R-L-C circuit for ac voltage input

A series R-L-C circuit has resistor(R), inductor(L) and capacitor(C) connected in parallel.Here the input voltage is sinusoidal. The current in the circuit is same at all points and is sinusoidal with phase shift. This circuit can be solved either mathematically or using phasor diagrams. A detailed derivation of the solution of the circuit using is given below.



Circuit analysis









Impedence
It refers to effective resistence offered by a component or a circuit to an alternating current.
For a capacitor, XC =-j/(Cw)
For an inductor, XL =Lwj
where w is the angular frequency of the ac current
Therefore the total impedence of the above circuit is:
Z = R+XC+XL = R+(Lw-1/Cw)j
|Z|= √ ((XL-XC)2+R2))
Circuit analysis
Applying kirchhoff's voltage law in the above circuit,
VL+VR+VC=Vmsin(wt)
Writing Vmsin(wt) and current as phasor, the above equation becomes:
ILwj+IR+-Ij/Cw =V∠ 0
where I is the current phasor and V is the voltage phasor.
|V|= Vrms which is the rms value of the input voltage, Vrms=Vm/√ 2
Therefore I=Vrms/|Z| ∠ -Θ where Θ =tan-1((Lw-1/Cw)/R) and |I|=Irms=Vrms/|Z|


Therefore I=Vrms/|Z| ∠ -Θ in phasor form and I(t)=(Vm/|Z|)sin(wt-Θ)
